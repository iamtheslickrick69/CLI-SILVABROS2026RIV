## Understanding JEA: Jacksonville's Municipal Utility and Solar

JEA (Jacksonville Electric Authority) is one of the largest community-owned electric utilities in the United States, serving over 500,000 customers across Duval County and parts of surrounding areas. Because JEA is municipally owned rather than investor-owned, its solar programs operate under a different framework than what homeowners in FPL or Duke Energy territory experience.

### What This Means for Solar Homeowners

- **Rate structures are set locally**, not by the Florida Public Service Commission. JEA has historically maintained competitive residential rates, but those rates have been increasing — making solar a stronger hedge against future hikes.
- **JEA has its own interconnection process** for solar installations, separate from the statewide rules governing FPL and Duke.
- **Billing and credit systems** are managed directly by JEA, which can make the process more straightforward for homeowners who want clarity about how their solar credits work.

Understanding JEA's specific policies is essential before going solar in Jacksonville. A qualified installer — like RIV Solar — will handle the JEA interconnection paperwork, permitting, and system design to ensure your installation complies with all municipal requirements.

## JEA SolarSmart Program: What Homeowners Should Know

JEA's **SolarSmart program** is the utility's framework for residential and commercial solar customers. Under this program, homeowners who install rooftop solar panels can connect their systems to the JEA grid and receive credits for excess electricity their panels produce.

### How JEA SolarSmart Works

1. **You install a solar panel system** on your home (typically 6-12 kW for residential properties in Jacksonville).
2. **Your system connects to JEA's grid** through an approved bidirectional meter.
3. **When your panels produce more electricity than you use**, the excess flows back to JEA and you receive a credit on your bill.
4. **When your panels are not producing** (at night, for example), you draw from the grid as usual.

### JEA Net Metering Credits

JEA's net metering policy allows solar customers to offset their electricity consumption with credits earned from excess solar production. Here are the key details Jacksonville homeowners should understand:

- **Credits are applied to your monthly JEA bill**, reducing what you owe.
- **System size limits** apply — JEA typically allows residential systems up to a certain capacity tied to your historical usage. Your installer will design a system that maximizes production within these guidelines.
- **The interconnection application** must be submitted and approved before your system goes live. RIV Solar handles this entire process as part of every Jacksonville installation.

The bottom line: JEA's solar program means Jacksonville homeowners can produce their own clean energy, reduce their dependence on grid electricity, and lower their monthly bills — all while maintaining a reliable grid connection for nighttime and cloudy-day usage.

Jacksonville's metro area spans an enormous geographic footprint, and solar potential varies somewhat by neighborhood. Here is a quick guide to some of the best areas for solar energy in the Jacksonville region.

## Permitting Solar Panels in Duval County

Every solar installation in Jacksonville requires permits from the City of Jacksonville / Duval County building department. Here is what homeowners should know about the permitting process.

### What the Permitting Process Involves

1. **Structural review.** The county verifies that your roof can support the additional load of solar panels (typically 3-5 pounds per square foot).
2. **Electrical review.** Your system's electrical design — including inverter type, wiring, and grid interconnection — must meet local and national electrical codes.
3. **Zoning compliance.** The installation must comply with local setback and placement requirements.
4. **Inspection.** After installation, a county inspector verifies that the system was installed according to the approved plans.

### How Long Does Permitting Take in Jacksonville?

Duval County permitting timelines vary, but homeowners should expect:

- **1-3 weeks for permit approval** after submission.
- **Inspection scheduling** within a few days of installation completion.
- **JEA interconnection approval** after the county inspection passes — typically an additional 1-2 weeks.

## Solar Incentives Available to Jacksonville Homeowners

Jacksonville homeowners benefit from a powerful stack of solar incentives that significantly reduce the cost of going solar.

### Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — 30%

The federal ITC allows you to claim 30% of your total solar installation cost as a dollar-for-dollar credit on your federal income taxes. For a $30,000 system, that is a $9,000 tax credit. This incentive is available through 2032 and applies to both purchased and financed systems.

### Florida Sales Tax Exemption

Solar energy equipment is 100% exempt from Florida's 6% sales tax. On a $30,000 system, that saves you $1,800 at the point of purchase — automatically.

### Florida Property Tax Exemption

The added home value from your solar panel system is 100% exempt from property taxes in Florida. Your home value goes up. Your property tax bill does not. In a high-growth area like Jacksonville where property values are already climbing, this exemption is increasingly valuable.

### JEA Net Metering Credits

As outlined above, excess solar energy sent back to JEA earns credits on your monthly bill. While this is not a direct rebate, it functions as an ongoing financial return on your solar investment for the life of your system.

### $0-Down Solar Financing

RIV Solar offers $0-down financing options that allow Jacksonville homeowners to go solar with no upfront cost. Your monthly solar payment replaces — and is typically lower than — your current JEA bill from day one.

When you stack the 30% federal tax credit, state tax exemptions, JEA net metering credits, and $0-down financing together, the math becomes very clear: going solar in Jacksonville costs less than continuing to pay JEA for grid electricity.
